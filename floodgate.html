Big M.O.M.M.A

Big M.O.M.M.A

This Boss spawns with 4 caster adds,

Darkfuse Mechadrone:

These should be focused down, will discuss why.
Maximum Distortion: If this goes through it will do an AoE damage burst with a knockback. Always kick this.
Shoot: Will constantly shoot random players for ST-damage.
Doom Storm: Draws a line of swirlies towards a player, the bot then charges in that direction.
To avoid too much spreading on the bots try to bait this into a wall if possible.

Now we will go over the boss-mechanics.

Electro Crush: Heavy tank-buster.
Sonic Boom: Targets a random player and sends out a large damaging orb towards them after a delay, try to aim this away from the rest of the pary.
Kill-o-Block Barrier:The boss becomes immune to all damage until the Darkfuse Mechadrone are killed.
INTERMISSION: When all Darkfuse Mechadrone are dead, the boss goes into a short intermission.
Jumpstart: Pulses heavy AoE damage to the party for 12s, and takes 2x damage.
When Jumpstart: finishes casting the boss will resummon Darkfuse Mechadrones, place a growing lightning field under itself and restart at P1.

Demolition Duo

Demolition Duo

A potentially very chaotic fight that revolves around dealing with the 6 Bombs the bosses spawn periodically.
Think of them like the bombs in the First Boss of Siege of Boralus, they will explode for massive AoE if they time out.
These are dealt with through the other mechanics of the boss.
A quick note is that this boss consists of 2 dudes, Quickfuse and Bront.
They don't share a healthpool and if one dies before the other, the survivor will get a ramping damage buff. So try to kill them evenly, but it isn't as punishing as third boss of SV for example.

Bomb stuff

Big Bada Boom: The boss spawns 6 Bombs around the arena.
Kinetic Explosive Gel: Quickfuse applies a dispellable magic debuff on someone.
The person with the debuff has an impact circle around them, if a bomb is inside the radius when the debuff is dispelled, the bomb will disappear.
This one of the main mechanics used for removing bombs so if you have the debuff stand next to a bomb and call for a dispel.
Barreling Charge: Bront targets a random (non-tank) and draws an arrow towards them.
After a delay he will charge in that direction, disabling any Bombs he runs through.
Bront will recast this twice, first on another random target, and finally again always on the tank.
Before this ability goes out Bront should be tanked in a spot where it makes it easy for the person with the charge to aim it into bombs.
Per bomb wave we have 2 casts of Kinetic Explosive Gel and 1 cast of Barreling Charge to get rid of them.

Wallop: Does what it says on the can.
Shoot:Quickfuse shoots random player.
B.B.B.F.C:Quickfuse does multiple frontal lines in a fan.

Swampface

Swampface

This boss seems simple on paper but is made hard by his tether mechanic.
On pull the boss will tether 2 dps players, and 1 dps + the healer together. Dealing constant rot damage whilst the tethers are up.
Also if the two tethered people move too far away from eachother, they will be snapped back together.
This becomes very troublesome with the rest of the boss' mechanics.
Sludge Claws: Heavy tankbuster.
Mudslide: Wide line frontal.
When this frontal comes out the group should have already decided which direction they should all dodge it in. Otherwise the tethers will snap you back into it.
Awaking the Swamp: Spawns large swirlies around the room that later explode, unleashing waves from the original swirlie.
When he explodes the swirlies he will also do large pulsing AoE damage to the entire group.
Again when these waves come out, everyone should be agreed in which direction to dodge to avoid snapping the tethers.
Razorchoke vines: Reapplies the tethers and then repeats previous mechanics.

Geezle Gigazap

Geezle Gigazap

This fight is all about managing your Pools of Water that periodically spawn throughout the fight.
These Pools of Water can nullify certain mechanics of the boss, but when used can't be used again.
Turbocharge: The boss spawns line frontals around him to be dodged for 8s and deals heavy pulsing AoE damage to the party.
Thunder Punch: A tank buster that also makes the tank Electrified.
Electrified players have a heavy DoT, this can be dispelled by walking into a Pool of Water but this will render the pool unusable.
Giga Zap: Targets a random player with a damaging circle which will deal heavy damage to everything around them and Electrify both the targeted player and anyone in the circle.
This can be dispelled in the same way as discussed before.
Leaping Sparks: The boss spawns an electrical spark for each player, which spark is yours is indicated, this spark will chase you and stun you if it connects.
However to nullify this you kite this spark into a Pool of Water which will make it disappear.
This renders the pool unusable in the future however and anyone coming into contact with an electrified pool will be stunned and take damage.
Important note: If you are standing in the Pools of Water as you kite a spark into it, you will be stunned, so try to position yourself behind the pool instead.
